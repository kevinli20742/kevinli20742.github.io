Academic Commentary on Hercules

Many of the ancient retellings, including the ones explored here, focus on the deep connection that Hercules has with divine intervention along with a focus on tragedy. In Hesiod’s Theogony, it is clear that many of the asides that are associated with Hercules are related to his physical feats that are almost always a result of Zeus’ or some other god’s will to maintain order in the world. Here, there is almost no individuality and most of the control lies in the gods that are more powerful than Hercules. In Sophocles’ Women of Trachis, the focus is on the tragedy of Hercules along with the humanity that he maintains despite being such a physically powerful hero. This mirrors Euripides’ Heracles which also focuses on the tragedy that unravels around Hercules but also puts emphasis once again on the divine intervention (Hera this time) as he goes mad and suffers the pain of killing his own family. Ovid’s Metamorphoses once more focuses on the tragedy behind Hercules as he kills his own family, and explores the humanity behind a figure that is deemed so powerful. Overall, much of what Hercules accomplishes and suffers from is a result of the decisions of the gods around him. Additionally, he is depicted as someone who is powerless against fate, and because of this suffers great tragedies emphasizing the message that the greatest heroes must succumb to divine forces.


Contrary to these ancient primary depictions, the modern retellings focus more on Hercules’ own virtues along with his personal development. In his Marvel and Disney retellings, the focus is very much on the personality and shortcomings of who he is as a person. The plot and storylines often revolve around how these shortcomings grow, and how he develops into a more virtuous person in order to fulfill his role as a hero. Beyond this, there is far less emphasis on divine intervention. In the Marvel retellings he goes so far as to disobey Zeus in order to do what he feels is right. Another instance of there being a focus on Hercules’ flaws on a personal level comes from Rick Riordan’s interpretation in the Heroes of Olympus series. Here, he is seen as a cocky and troubled individual (as a result of going through the twelve labors) which is the main focus for Percy Jackson and his crew. In the most recent retelling that is explored, The Rock’s 2014 Hercules adaptation, the entire movie focuses on Hercules being a hero through his bravery and resilience despite not having help from the gods. This version of Hercules is great because he is merely a mortal, emphasizing this focus on personal virtues and Hercules’ own development as a person.


There is a clear shift from the ancient versions of Hercules to his more modern retellings. There is a dramatic difference in the focus on divine intervention and resulting tragedy compared to the exploration of virtues and personal development. This largely reflects broader changes in cultural values. Ancient societies oftentimes emphasized the fragility of humans in light of divine forces like the gods while modern society is far more focused on the individual. Today, there is so much focus on personal growth and development, a theme that is present in so many modern stories, so it only makes sense that Hercules is depicted in that way as well.


I originally came up with this idea while reading through The Odyssey as I wanted the opportunity to explore more heroes. My idea was first to explore how all heroes have changed through time, but upon narrowing the scope of the project, I chose to focus more on Hercules, a hero with many modern retellings. In terms of the medium, I saw many examples of paintings and pottery, and since I study ECE and am going into a software engineering career, I thought it would be fitting to put those skills to use with this project. I first started by making a skeleton for the website so I could fill in the actual content later. This was one of the most enjoyable parts of the project since I had all the creative freedom to model my website however I wanted to. Next, I did research from my eight sources on a document and made edits wherever needed. The last step was just to put these two together and form this final version of the website. In the future, I think a worthy topic to explore is still the concept of how all ancient heroes have evolved over time. It would be really interesting to see if there are differences in these evolutions, or if there is a common theme across all of them.
